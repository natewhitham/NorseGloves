About

NorseGlovesMW was estabilished in the summer of 2015. Since then NorseGlovesMW has grown to have over 1.5K followers.


NorseGloveMW was founded by two Division III college baseball player from Luther College in Decorah, IA. Since then, one of the owners has since backed out after suffering a serious arm injury. The account is now ran solely by me, Nate Whitham.


A little bit about myself and my baseball background. I am a 2012 high school graduate from the state of Iowa. I then went on to play baseball for Luther College in the fall of 2012. In 2013, as a freshman, I finished second in the conference with a 1.95 ERA in 37 innings. After the season I returned home to pitch in a summer league. After my first outting, I walked away with a sore arm and did not pitch again for the rest of the summer.


Upon returning to school in the fall I was still not 100%, and I rehabbed the whole fall and winter to be ready for when the season started. When the season rolled around, I was still not 100% myself, but I still managed to log 24 2/3 innings. After the season, I went under the knife for a suprascapular nerve decompression. Meaning the nerve that leads to the muscle on your scapula was being pinched by a ligament. This injury is fairly uncommon injury, so going in I knew the surgery may not solve my issue.




After undergoing the surgery, I was able to begin rehab to get back to throwing in time to be ready for my junior season. Ultimately, I was never the same player that I was my freshman year and logged a total of 16 inning my junior and senior year combined.


In my junior and senior year we made our conference tournament, after not making it as a freshman and sophomore. My senior season we finished 3rd in the regular season, and swept our conference tournament to make it to regionals. We ended the season with a 34-14 record, setting the school record for wins in a season. We won more games my senior year than we did my freshman and sophomore year combined.


After my playing career ended, I returned to Luther College in the fall of 2016 to help coach during the fall season. Currently, I am an assisstant volunteer sophomre level coach at my high school while still trying to finish my degree.
